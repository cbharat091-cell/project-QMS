# Medical Device Quality Management System Procedure

## Document Control
- Title: Medical Device Quality Management System Procedure
- Version: 2.0
- Effective Date: 2024-03-01
- Document Owner: Quality Manager - Medical Devices
- Approved By: Chief Regulatory Officer
- Reviewed By: Regulatory Affairs

## Purpose and Scope
This procedure establishes the Quality Management System for medical device manufacturing in compliance with ISO 13485:2016 and FDA 21 CFR Part 820. This procedure applies to all medical device design, development, production, and distribution activities.

## Regulatory Framework
Our QMS complies with:
- ISO 13485:2016 - Medical devices - Quality management systems
- FDA 21 CFR Part 820 - Quality System Regulation
- ISO 14971:2019 - Application of risk management to medical devices
- EU Medical Device Regulation (MDR) 2017/745

## Design and Development
### Design Planning
Design and development planning includes defining design and development stages, review points, responsibilities, and interfaces. Design inputs are documented and include performance requirements, regulatory requirements, and user needs.

### Design Output
Design output specifications include drawings, specifications, software code, and manufacturing instructions. All design outputs are reviewed and approved before release.

### Design Verification
Design verification confirms that design outputs meet design input requirements. Testing protocols and results are documented.

### Design Validation
Design validation is performed under defined conditions using production units or their equivalents. Clinical evaluations and usability studies are conducted as appropriate.

### Design Changes
All design changes are documented, reviewed, and approved before implementation.

## Production and Process Controls
### Production Control
Production is conducted in accordance with documented procedures and instructions. Work orders specify the product, quantity, and special requirements.

### Process Validation
Process validation demonstrates that established processes consistently produce conforming products. IQ, OQ, and PQ protocols are executed for critical processes.

### Production Equipment
Equipment qualification includes installation qualification (IQ), operational qualification (OQ), and performance qualification (PQ). Preventive maintenance schedules are established.

### Environmental Controls
Environmental conditions are controlled and monitored. Temperature, humidity, and particulate counts are maintained within specified limits for cleanroom operations.

### Product Identification and Traceability
Each product or lot is identified with unique device identifier (UDI). Traceability records enable reconstruction of the manufacturing history.

## Risk Management
Risk management activities are conducted per ISO 14971:2019. Risk analysis identifies hazards and estimates risk. Risk evaluation assesses acceptability of identified risks.

### Risk Control
Risk control measures include inherent safety design, protective measures, and information for safety. Residual risk is evaluated after risk controls are implemented.

### Risk Management File
A risk management file is maintained documenting all risk management activities. Benefits of risk control are weighed against residual risks.

## Medical Device Reporting
### Adverse Event Reporting
Adverse events are reported to regulatory authorities within required timeframes. Serious injuries and deaths are reported to FDA per 21 CFR Part 803.

### Complaint Handling
Complaints are documented, investigated, and trended. Root cause analysis is performed for投诉 related to product quality.

### CAPA and Nonconformance
Nonconforming product is identified, documented, and evaluated. Corrective actions address root cause of nonconformances. Preventive actions avoid recurrence. CAPA effectiveness is verified.

## Labeling and Packaging
### Labeling Requirements
Labels include product name, manufacturer information, UDI, and intended use. Label integrity is verified throughout shelf life.

### Packaging
Packaging maintains sterility and protection during shipping. Package integrity testing is performed.

## Records and Retention
### Quality Records
All quality records are maintained including design files, production records, test results, and traceability records. Records are legible and retrievable.

### Retention Period
Records are retained for the longer of: the lifetime of the device, or two years after the last commercial distribution. Device history records (DHR) are retained per regulatory requirements.

## Roles and Responsibilities
### Quality Manager
The Quality Manager has overall responsibility for the Quality Management System implementation and maintenance. The Quality Manager ensures that processes are documented and comply with regulatory requirements. The Quality Manager has the authority to stop production when quality issues arise.

### Production Manager
The Production Manager is responsible for production operations and ensuring that products are manufactured according to approved procedures. The Production Manager coordinates with the Quality Manager on quality matters and ensures personnel are trained.

### Regulatory Affairs
Regulatory Affairs is responsible for ensuring compliance with applicable regulations including FDA 21 CFR Part 820 and ISO 13485. Regulatory Affairs maintains communications with regulatory authorities and oversees submissions.

### Design Team
The design team is responsible for design and development activities per approved design plans. The design team ensures design inputs are addressed and design outputs meet requirements. Design reviews are conducted at defined stages with responsible persons identified.

### Document Control
Document Control is responsible for maintaining controlled documents, managing revisions, and ensuring document availability. All documents are reviewed and approved by authorized personnel before release.

## Artificial Intelligence (AI) Governance

### AI Implementation and Control
Artificial Intelligence (AI) utilized within the organization or integrated into the company website is implemented in a controlled and compliant manner under this Quality Management System. All AI-driven functionality, including automated customer interaction, data analytics, or decision-support tools, is subject to documented validation, risk assessment, and change control procedures.

### Regulatory Compliance
AI systems comply with:
- ISO 13485:2016 - Quality management system requirements for medical devices
- IVDR (In Vitro Diagnostic Regulation) 2017/745 - For AI-powered IVD devices
- Applicable cybersecurity standards including IEC 62443
- Data privacy regulations including GDPR and HIPAA

### AI Validation and Risk Assessment
All AI systems undergo rigorous validation before deployment. Risk assessment is conducted per ISO 14971 to identify hazards associated with AI functionality. Risk evaluation considers AI-specific risks including algorithmic bias, model drift, and output reliability.

### Change Control for AI Systems
Changes to AI algorithms, models, or underlying data are controlled through the documented change control procedure. All AI modifications require:
- Impact assessment
- Regression testing
- Validation updates
- Authorized approval

### AI Output Review
AI outputs are reviewed where necessary to prevent misleading regulatory or product claims. Human oversight is maintained for decisions with regulatory implications. Review procedures ensure accuracy and compliance of AI-generated content.

### Protection of Regulatory Information
No automated system is permitted to modify regulatory information including CE marking, IVDR status, or WHO Prequalification status without authorized review and approval. All regulatory claims generated by AI are verified by qualified personnel before publication.

### Cybersecurity and Data Privacy
Data privacy, cybersecurity, and system integrity controls are maintained to ensure protection of confidential information and regulatory compliance. AI systems implement:
- Access controls and authentication
- Data encryption
- Audit trails
- Vulnerability management
- Incident response procedures

### Machine Learning Model Management
Machine learning models are managed throughout their lifecycle including training, deployment, monitoring, and retirement. Model performance is continuously monitored for drift and accuracy degradation. Retraining procedures follow validated processes.
